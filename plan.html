Plan Your Magical Winter Adventure

Everything you need to know about visiting the land of snow, silence, and the Northern Lights.

When to Visit

Discover the unique seasons of the Finnish winter.

dark_mode

The Polar Night (Kaamos)

From December to mid-January, the sun doesn't rise above the horizon. Instead, experience a magical "blue hour" where the snow reflects soft, ethereal light. It's the coziest time for log cabins and candles.

ac_unit Best for cozy vibes
flare

Northern Lights Season

February and March offer the clearest skies and longer daylight hours for activities. The Aurora Borealis is most active around the equinox, dancing across the dark Arctic skies in vibrant greens and purples.

auto_awesome Best for Aurora spotting
wb_sunny

March – Spring Lapland

The snow is still thick but daylight returns. Perfect for skiing, snowmobiling, and outdoor photography. Temperatures are milder, making it great for families with children.

downhill_skiing Best for outdoor sports
celebration

December – Christmas Magic

The most festive time! Santa's village is alive with activity, markets are glowing with lights, and everyone is celebrating. The ultimate holiday experience for al the family.

holiday_village Best for families

How to Get There

flight

Fly to Helsinki or Rovaniemi

Main hubs with international connections. Finnair offers daily flights into the heart of Lapland.

train

The Santa Claus Express

A magical overnight sleeper train from Helsinki to Rovaniemi and Kemijärvi.

directions_car

Arctic Driving

Rent a car with winter tires. Roads are well-maintained, but watch for reindeer crossings!

directions_bus

Coach & Bus Services

Matkahuolto operates extensive bus routes connecting all major Finnish cities at affordable prices.

Base Layers

Merino wool is best for moisture-wicking.

Fleece or down jackets to trap heat.

Useful Information

health_and_safety

Travel Insurance

Always purchase comprehensive travel and health insurance before your trip. EU citizens can use EHIC cards.

currency_exchange

Currency

Finland uses the Euro (€). Cards are widely accepted everywhere, even in small villages. Cash is rarely needed.

language

Language

Finnish and Swedish are official languages. English is widely spoken, especially in tourist areas and cities.
